Company Law
A-Z

Account

1). A report or description of an event or experience. 2). A record or statement of financial expenditure or receipts relating to a particular period or purpose. 3). An arrangement by which a user is given personalized access to a computer, website, or application, typically by entering a username and password.


Administration

1). The process or activity of running a business, organization, etc. 2). The officials in the executive branch of government under a particular chief executive. 3). The action of dispensing, giving, or applying something.


Amalgamation

An amalgamation is a combination of two or more companies into a new entity. Amalgamation is distinct from a merger because neither company involved survives as a legal entity. Instead, a completely new entity is formed to house the combined assets and liabilities of both companies.


Appointment

1). An arrangement to meet someone at a particular time and place. 2). An act of appointing; assigning a job or position to someone.


Asset

Generally, any item of property that has monetary value, including articles with only sentimental value (particularly in the estates of the dead). Assets are shown in balance sheets of businesses and inventories of probate estates. There are current assets (which includes accounts receivable), fixed assets (basic equipment and structures), and such intangibles as business good will and rights to market a product.


Association

Any group of people who have joined together for a particular purpose, ranging from social to business, and usually meant to be a continuing organization.


Deal

An agreement entered into by two or more parties for their mutual benefits, especially in a business or political context.


Delegation

A body of delegates or representatives; a deputation.


Director

A member of the governing board of a corporation or association elected or re-elected at annual meetings of the shareholders or members.


Eligibility

The state of having the right to do or obtain something through satisfaction of the appropriate conditions.


Employee

A person who is hired for a wage, salary, fee or payment to perform work for an employer.


Fiduciary

Involving trust, especially with regard to the relationship between a trustee and a beneficiary.


Fraudulent

Obtained, done by, or involving deception, especially criminal deception.


Guarantee

1) To pledge or agree to be responsible for another's debt or contractual performance if that other person does not pay or perform. Usually, the party receiving the guarantee will first try to collect or obtain performance from the debtor before trying to collect from the one making the guarantee (guarantor). 2) The promise to pay another's debt or fulfill contract obligations if that party fails to pay or perform. 3) Occasionally, the person to whom the guarantee is made. 4) A promise to make a product good if it has some defect.


Imprisonment

The state of being imprisoned; captivity.


Insolvent

Unable to pay debts owed.


Invoice

A list of goods sent or services provided, with a statement of the sum due for these; a bill.


Limited Company

A general form of incorporation that limits the amount of liability undertaken by the company's shareholders. It refers to a legal structure that ensures that the liability of company members or subscribers is limited to their stake in the company by way of investments or commitments.


Management

The process of dealing with or controlling things or people.


Offence

A breach of a law or rule; an illegal act.


Offshore Company

It is a company that one has filed outside of the country where its principals reside. The principals include the officers, directors, shareholders, members, partners. From within its country of formation, it is a company that has been formed for the purpose of operating outside of the jurisdiction where it was originally filed.


Oppressive

Inflicting harsh and authoritarian treatment.


Perpetual

(of an investment) having no fixed maturity date; irredeemable.


Pre-incorporation Contract

A contract where one party of the contract is a company that is yet to be incorporated.


Private Company

A company whose shares may not be offered to the public for sale and which operates under legal requirements less strict than those for a public company.


Provision

A condition or requirement in a legal document.


Public Company

A company whose shares are traded freely on a stock exchange.


Reckless

Heedless of danger or the consequences of one's actions; rash or impetuous.


Registrar

An official responsible for keeping a register or official records.


Reliance

Dependence on or trust in someone or something.


Rely

Depend on with full trust or confidence.


Remedy

Set right (an undesirable situation).


Remuneration

Money paid for work or a service.


Restriction

A limiting condition or measure, especially a legal one.


Share

1) A portion of a benefit from a trust, estate, claim or business usually in equal division (or a specifically stated fraction) with others ("to my three daughters, in equal shares"). 2) A portion of ownership interest in a corporation, represented by a stock certificate stating the number of shares of an issue of the corporation's stock.


Share Register

A record of a company's shareholders which lists the names and addresses of shareholders and the number of shares and other company securities held by them.


Shareholder

An owner of shares in a company.


Subsequent Director

A Non-Employee Director of the company who becomes a member of the Board (or with respect to directors who are also employees of the company, a director who becomes a Non-Employee Director) subsequent to the effective date.


The Board

Collectively directors are called ‘the Board’


Tort

A wrongful act or an infringement of a right (other than under contract) leading to legal liability.


Unanimous

Fully in agreement of two or more people.


Unlimited Company

A regular private company limited by shares. It must be registered with Companies House and have a memorandum and articles of association. There's a director that manages the day-to-day running of the company on behalf of the shareholders. Persons of significant control and an annual confirmation statement must still be submitted to Companies House.
