Sources of Law
A-Z

Abrogate

To annul or repeal a law or pass legislation that contradicts the prior law. Abrogate also applies to revoking or withdrawing conditions of a contract.


Acquired Property

Any real or personal property, plant, building, facility, structure, underground storage tank, equipment or unit or other asset used in the Acquired Business or otherwise owned, operated, leased, or used by the Acquired Entities.


Adoption

The action or fact of legally taking another's child and bringing it up as one's own, or the fact of being adopted.


Amendment

A minor change or addition designed to improve a text, piece of legislation, etc.


Appellate Court

A court of appeals which hears appeals from lower court decisions. The term is often used in legal briefs to describe a court of appeals.


Authority

1). The power or right to give orders, make decisions, and enforce obedience. 2). A person or organization having political or administrative power and control.


Certainty

Firm conviction that something in the case.


Conquered

Having been overcome and taken control of by military force.


Continuity

1). The unbroken and consistent existence or operation of something over a period of time. 2). The maintenance of continuous action and self-consistent detail in the various scenes of a movie or broadcast.


Custom

1). Regular dealings with a shop or business by customers. 2). Made or done to order for a particular customer.


Deed

A legal document that is signed and delivered, especially one regarding the ownership of property or legal rights.


Doctrine

A belief or set of beliefs held and taught by a church, political party, or other group.


Domicile

The place where a person has his/her permanent principal home to which he/she returns or intends to return.


Donation

Gift. If made to a qualified non-profit charitable, religious, educational or public service organization, it may be deductible as a contribution in calculating income tax.


Dowry

An amount of property or money brought by a bride to her husband on their marriage.


Heirloom

A valuable object that has belonged to a family for several generations.


Immovable Property

Immovable property is property that cannot be moved from one place to another. It is generally connected to the ground or land on which it sits. The term immovable property also includes the land.


Indigenous Law

The laws of indigenous people’s treaties between indigenous groups and the Crown (government) government laws that create rights and obligations specifically and only affecting indigenous people.


Inhabitant

A person or animal that lives in or occupies a place.


Inheritance

A thing that is inherited.


Intermarry

Become connected by marriage.


Intestacy

The condition of an estate of a person who dies without a will, and owns property with a total value greater than that of their outstanding debts. In addition, a will that covers only part of an estate sometimes is intestate.


Judicial Precedent

Lower courts have to follow the decisions of higher courts. This is called judicial precedent, binding precedent or precedent.


Lawful

Conforming to, permitted by, or recognized by law or rules.


Legislature

The legislative body of a country or state.


Maternal

Relating to a mother, especially during pregnancy or shortly after childbirth.


Monarch

A sovereign head of state, especially a king, queen, or emperor.


Mortgage

A legal agreement by which a bank, building society, etc. lends money at interest in exchange for taking title of the debtor's property, with the condition that the conveyance of title becomes void upon the payment of the debt.


Mortgagee

The person or business making a loan that is secured by the real property of the person (mortgagor) who owes him/her/its money.


Movable Property

Property that you own and can take with you, which does not include houses, apartments, or land.


Pagan

A person holding religious beliefs other than those of the main world religions.


Paternal

Of or appropriate to a father.


Pawn

To pledge an item of personal property as security for a loan, with the property left with the pawnbroker. The interest rates are on the high side, the amount of the loan is well below the value of the pledged property, and the broker has the right to sell the item without further notice if the loan is not paid. Pawnbrokers are licensed by the state.


Pawnbroker

A person who lends money at interest on the security of an article pawned.


Per Incurian Rule

Per incuriam, literally translated as "through lack of care", refers to a judgment of a court which has been decided without reference to a statutory provision or earlier judgment which would have been relevant.


Portion

The part or share of an estate given or descending by law to an heir.


Prerogative

The right of the sovereign, which in British law is theoretically subject to no restriction.


Revoke

Officially cancel (a decree, decision, or promise).


Sovereign

A supreme ruler, especially a monarch.


Star Decisis

The legal principle of determining points in litigation according to precedent.


Statute Law

The body of principles and rules of law laid down in statutes.


Statutory Instrument

A government or executive order of subordinate legislation.


Superior Court

1). A court of appeals or a court of general jurisdiction. 2). A court with general jurisdiction over other courts; a higher court. 3). The name used in 16 states for the basic county trial court.
